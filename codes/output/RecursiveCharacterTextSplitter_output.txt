Layer 2 — Retrieval / Memory Layer
What it is
LLMs have no persistent memory — every API call starts from scratch. The retrieval/memory layer solves this. It
stores information as vector embeddings (numerical representations of meaning) and retrieves the most
semantically relevant pieces at query time. This is the technology behind RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation):
the technique that lets an AI answer questions about your private documents, codebase, or knowledge base.
Your role as an AI engineer
You design the indexing pipeline (how documents are chunked, embedded, and stored) and the retrieval strategy
(how the most relevant chunks are fetched at query time). You choose the right vector database, embedding model,
and chunking strategy for your specific use case. You also set up semantic caching here to avoid redundant LLM
calls.
